This scatter plot dynamically evaluates US data health indicators. Select the X and Y axis variables to view how they relate to each other.

Analysis:

  • There is a relatively linear correlation between obesity and poverty, with that correlation increasing (or at least a reduction in outliers) between smoking and poverty.
  • The median age tends to fall between 35 and 40, with Utah being the biggest outlier.
  • There is an inverse correlation between lacking healthcare and median income. As income goes up, so does the % of those with healthcare, while the % of smokers decrease along with rates of obesity.

Generalizations

Utah stands out as an outlier:
  • The lowest % of smokers, youngest median age, relatively low poverty with high median income (not skewed to fewer wealthy individuals).
  • One would assume that this is attributable to also correlative data of cohesive family units and above average education.
DC and CO:
  • They have very similar rates of obesity and smoking, and tend to maintain about the same distance from each other.
  • A detailed breakdown of population demographics would likely lead to some striking similarities.
